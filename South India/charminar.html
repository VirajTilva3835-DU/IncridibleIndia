Charminar, Hyderabad (Telangana)

"A symbol of Hyderabad — blending history, culture, and Islamic architecture."

About the Charminar

The Charminar, located in the heart of Hyderabad, Telangana, is one of the most recognized landmarks in India. Built in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, the fifth ruler of the Qutb Shahi dynasty, it marks the founding of the city of Hyderabad.


The name "Charminar" literally means "Four Minarets", referring to its four grand towers that rise gracefully from the corners of the structure. The monument is constructed in Indo-Islamic architectural style with influences of Persian design, making it a timeless masterpiece.


Interesting Facts

  • The Charminar was built to commemorate the eradication of a deadly plague in the region.
  • It stands at the intersection of Hyderabad’s main historical trade routes.
  • Each minaret is 48.7 meters (160 feet) tall and has a spiral staircase of 149 steps.
  • It is surrounded by the famous Laad Bazaar and Makkah Masjid.
  • The structure glows beautifully when illuminated at night.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Visit early morning or evening for pleasant weather and better photos.
  • Climb up the minaret for a panoramic view of the Old City (entry permitted in parts).
  • Shop for bangles and pearls at the nearby Laad Bazaar.
  • Nearby attractions: Makkah Masjid, Chowmahalla Palace, and Salar Jung Museum.
