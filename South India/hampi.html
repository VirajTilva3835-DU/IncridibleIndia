Hampi, Karnataka

"UNESCO World Heritage site with ancient ruins of Vijayanagara Empire and boulder landscapes."

About Hampi

Hampi is an ancient village in Karnataka, recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It was once the glorious capital of the Vijayanagara Empire during the 14th–16th centuries, one of the richest and largest empires in Indian history.


Today, Hampi stands as a magnificent open-air museum, with vast stretches of ruins, temples, and stone structures surrounded by surreal boulder-strewn landscapes. The town’s remarkable blend of nature and history attracts travelers, history enthusiasts, and photographers from around the globe.


Interesting Facts

  • Hampi was once the second-largest city in the world during the Vijayanagara Empire’s peak.
  • The iconic Stone Chariot in the Vittala Temple complex is featured on the ₹50 Indian currency note.
  • Over 1,600 monuments are spread across 26 square kilometers.
  • The Virupaksha Temple is one of the oldest functioning temples in India.
  • Hampi hosts the famous Hampi Utsav, celebrating its cultural legacy every year.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Start your exploration early morning to avoid heat and crowds.
  • Carry water, hats, and comfortable shoes for exploring ruins on foot.
  • Visit the Matanga Hill for breathtaking sunrise and sunset views.
  • Nearby attractions include Anjanadri Hill, Tungabhadra Dam, and Badami Caves.
