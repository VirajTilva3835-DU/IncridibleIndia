Sanchi Stupa, Madhya Pradesh

"Ancient Buddhist monument built by Emperor Ashoka, known for its stone carvings."

About Sanchi Stupa

The Sanchi Stupa is one of the oldest stone structures in India, commissioned by Emperor Ashoka the Great in the 3rd century BCE. Located in Sanchi, Madhya Pradesh, it is a major Buddhist monument and a symbol of peace, enlightenment, and architectural brilliance.


The Great Stupa at Sanchi houses sacred relics of Lord Buddha and features intricately carved gateways known as Toranas. Each torana depicts scenes from the life of Buddha, Jataka tales, and various symbolic motifs reflecting Buddhist teachings and philosophy.


Interesting Facts

  • Commissioned by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE.
  • The structure is a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1989.
  • Originally built with bricks and later enlarged with stone during the Shunga period.
  • The four gateways represent Love, Peace, Trust, and Courage.
  • The stupa symbolizes the path to enlightenment in Buddhism.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Visit early morning for peaceful atmosphere and soft lighting for photography.
  • Read the inscriptions and carvings on the toranas for historical insight.
  • Carry water and sun protection during summer visits.
  • Nearby attractions: Udayagiri Caves, Vidisha, and Bhopal's Buddhist Museum.
