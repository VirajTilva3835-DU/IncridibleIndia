Khajuraho Temples, Madhya Pradesh

"Exquisite group of Hindu and Jain temples famed for their detailed carvings and sculptures."

About Khajuraho Temples

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments is a collection of exquisite Hindu and Jain temples located in Chhatarpur district of Madhya Pradesh. Built between 950 and 1050 AD by the Chandela dynasty, these temples are famous for their stunning architecture, intricate sculptures, and symbolic artwork.


Out of the original 85 temples, around 25 have survived, each showcasing a remarkable combination of spirituality, art, and craftsmanship. The temples are divided into three groups — Western, Eastern, and Southern — each featuring elaborate carvings depicting gods, goddesses, celestial beings, animals, and daily life scenes.


Interesting Facts

  • Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1986.
  • Built by the Chandela rulers to represent the harmony between religion and art.
  • The sculptures are not only sensual but also symbolize spiritual enlightenment and human emotions.
  • The temples are aligned east-west, allowing sunlight to illuminate the deities inside.
  • The Kandariya Mahadev Temple is the largest and most ornate among them.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Attend the Khajuraho Dance Festival held every February–March.
  • Hire a local guide to understand the symbolism behind the carvings.
  • Photography is allowed, but drone usage requires special permission.
  • Nearby attractions: Panna National Park, Raneh Falls, and Ajaigarh Fort.
