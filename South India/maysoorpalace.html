Mysore Palace, Karnataka

"Opulent royal residence blending Indo-Saracenic architecture, lit beautifully at night."

About Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is a stunning royal residence in Mysuru, Karnataka. It served as the official seat of the Wadiyar dynasty, rulers of the Kingdom of Mysore. The palace showcases a majestic blend of Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic styles, a hallmark of Indo-Saracenic architecture.


Known for its grand interiors, intricate carvings, and the dazzling illumination of over 100,000 bulbs at night, Mysore Palace is one of India’s most visited monuments, drawing millions of tourists annually.


Interesting Facts

  • The current palace was rebuilt in 1912 after the old wooden structure was destroyed by fire in 1897.
  • It is illuminated with around 100,000 bulbs every Sunday and during festivals like Dussehra.
  • The palace was designed by British architect Henry Irwin.
  • Mysore Dussehra celebrations feature a royal procession that starts from the palace gates.
  • It is one of the most visited heritage sites in India, second only to the Taj Mahal.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Visit in the evening to witness the palace illuminated with thousands of lights.
  • Footwear must be removed before entering certain palace sections.
  • Photography is restricted inside some halls—check signage before clicking.
  • Nearby attractions include Chamundi Hills, St. Philomena’s Church, and Brindavan Gardens.
