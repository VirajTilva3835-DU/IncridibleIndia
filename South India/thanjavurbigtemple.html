Thanjavur Big Temple, Tamil Nadu

"A magnificent Chola-era temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, showcasing Dravidian architectural brilliance."

About Thanjavur Big Temple

The Brihadeeswarar Temple, also known as the Thanjavur Big Temple, is one of India’s greatest architectural marvels. Built by the Chola King Rajaraja Chola I in the 11th century, the temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and stands as a symbol of Tamil culture, engineering brilliance, and devotion.


Constructed entirely of granite, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is part of the “Great Living Chola Temples.” Its towering Vimana (temple tower) rises nearly 66 meters, making it one of the tallest in the world. The temple showcases exquisite sculptures, inscriptions, and murals that narrate the grandeur of the Chola dynasty.


Interesting Facts

  • The temple’s 80-ton granite capstone was lifted to the top without modern machinery — an architectural wonder of its time.
  • The Nandi (bull) statue at the entrance is carved from a single stone and weighs around 25 tons.
  • The temple tower casts no shadow on the ground at noon — a fascinating architectural feat.
  • It was built over 1,000 years ago (completed in 1010 CE).
  • It is one of the largest temples dedicated to Lord Shiva in the world.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Visit early morning or evening to avoid heat and crowds.
  • Photography is allowed in the outer areas but restricted inside sanctum.
  • Dress modestly as it’s an active place of worship.
  • Nearby attractions: Thanjavur Palace, Saraswathi Mahal Library, and Schwartz Church.
