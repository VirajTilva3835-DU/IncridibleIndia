Kanha National Park, Madhya Pradesh

"Dense forest reserve known for tigers, leopards, and barasingha — inspiration for The Jungle Book."

About Kanha National Park

Kanha National Park, located in the heart of Madhya Pradesh, is one of India’s most famous and well-maintained tiger reserves. Spanning over 940 sq km, it is known for its dense sal and bamboo forests, meadows, and abundant wildlife. The park inspired Rudyard Kipling’s iconic novel, The Jungle Book.


Established in 1955 and declared a Tiger Reserve in 1973, Kanha is home to the Royal Bengal Tiger, barasingha (swamp deer), leopards, wild dogs, sloth bears, and over 300 bird species. Its conservation success story for the barasingha is one of the most remarkable in India.


Interesting Facts

  • Served as the inspiration for Rudyard Kipling’s The Jungle Book.
  • Home to the rare and endangered barasingha (swamp deer), also called the “jewel of Kanha”.
  • Part of India’s first group of Project Tiger reserves launched in 1973.
  • Features beautiful meadows like Sonf Meadows and Bamni Dadar (Sunset Point).
  • Known for successful wildlife conservation and eco-tourism initiatives.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Book your jungle safari online in advance; morning safaris offer better sightings.
  • Wear earthy or neutral-colored clothes during safaris.
  • Carry binoculars and a good zoom lens for birdwatching.
  • Nearby attractions: Bamni Dadar, Kawardha Palace, and Bandhavgarh National Park.
