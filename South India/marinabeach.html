Marina Beach, Chennai (Tamil Nadu)

"One of the world’s longest beaches, famous for its golden sands, sunsets, and local snacks."

About Marina Beach

Marina Beach, located along the Bay of Bengal in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, is one of the longest urban beaches in the world, stretching over 13 kilometers. Known for its soft golden sands, cool sea breeze, and vibrant atmosphere, it is a favorite hangout for locals and tourists alike.


Apart from its natural beauty, the beach is lined with historical landmarks, memorials, and statues of prominent Indian leaders. During sunrise and sunset, Marina Beach offers breathtaking views, making it a must-visit spot for photographers and travelers.


Interesting Facts

  • Marina Beach is India’s longest natural urban beach, spanning around 13 km.
  • Swimming is prohibited due to strong undercurrents, but beach walks are very popular.
  • The beach features statues of national leaders, poets, and freedom fighters.
  • It attracts nearly 30,000 visitors every day and over 50,000 on weekends.
  • Iconic landmarks like Anna Memorial and MGR Memorial are located here.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Visit early morning or evening to enjoy cool breezes and avoid heat.
  • Try local snacks like sundal and murukku from beachside stalls.
  • Avoid swimming due to strong tides.
  • Nearby attractions: Santhome Cathedral, Kapaleeshwarar Temple, and Vivekananda House.
