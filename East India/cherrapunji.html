Cherrapunji, Meghalaya

"One of the wettest places on Earth, home to living root bridges and misty waterfalls."

About Cherrapunji

Perched on the southern edge of the Khasi Hills, Cherrapunji (also known as Sohra) is renowned as one of the wettest places on Earth. The town is a breathtaking blend of deep valleys, mist-covered cliffs, and countless waterfalls that descend dramatically into lush gorges.


Famous for its unique living root bridges, created by weaving the aerial roots of rubber trees, Cherrapunji is a marvel of human ingenuity and harmony with nature. The region offers awe-inspiring views, especially during the monsoon when the clouds descend upon the green landscape.


Interesting Facts

  • Cherrapunji once held the record for the highest annual rainfall in the world.
  • The locals, the Khasi tribe, build living root bridges that can last for centuries.
  • Nearby village Mawsynram now often surpasses Cherrapunji in rainfall.
  • Home to spectacular waterfalls like Nohkalikai Falls — India’s tallest plunge waterfall.
  • Cherrapunji sits on a plateau overlooking the plains of Bangladesh.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Carry rain gear — showers can occur anytime of the year.
  • Visit Nohkalikai Falls early morning for clear views.
  • Wear comfortable shoes for trekking to Double Decker Living Root Bridge.
  • Nearby attractions: Mawsynram, Dawki, Mawlynnong (Asia’s Cleanest Village).
