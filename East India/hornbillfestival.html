Hornbill Festival, Nagaland

"Cultural extravaganza celebrating Naga heritage, music, dance, and food."

About the Hornbill Festival

The Hornbill Festival, held annually in Kohima, Nagaland, is one of India’s most vibrant cultural events. Celebrated every December, it showcases the diverse heritage, traditions, crafts, and cuisine of Nagaland’s 16 major tribes. Named after the revered Hornbill bird, the festival promotes unity among Naga tribes and preserves their ancient customs.


Organized by the Government of Nagaland since 2000, the festival takes place at the picturesque Kisama Heritage Village. Visitors can experience traditional dance performances, music concerts, indigenous games, art exhibitions, and a colorful display of tribal attire — all set amidst the scenic hills of Nagaland.


Interesting Facts

  • Known as the “Festival of Festivals,” uniting all tribes of Nagaland in one grand celebration.
  • Held annually from December 1 to December 10 at Kisama Heritage Village.
  • Features traditional sports like wrestling, archery, and indigenous games.
  • Showcases Naga cuisine, handloom, and handicraft stalls.
  • Attracts thousands of domestic and international tourists each year.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Book accommodation early — hotels fill up quickly during the festival.
  • Try traditional dishes like smoked pork, bamboo shoot curry, and millet beer.
  • Carry warm clothes — temperatures can drop below 10°C in December.
  • Nearby attractions: Kohima War Cemetery, Dzükou Valley, and Japfu Peak.
