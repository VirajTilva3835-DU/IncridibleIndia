Victoria Memorial, Kolkata

"Marble monument built in memory of Queen Victoria, surrounded by scenic gardens."

About Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial in Kolkata, West Bengal, is an architectural masterpiece built between 1906 and 1921 in memory of Queen Victoria. Designed by Sir William Emerson, it showcases a blend of British and Mughal architectural styles, constructed entirely of white Makrana marble.


Surrounded by lush gardens spread over 64 acres, the memorial houses a large museum with more than 25 galleries displaying British-era artifacts, paintings, sculptures, and manuscripts. It stands as one of Kolkata’s most iconic landmarks and a reminder of India’s colonial past.


Interesting Facts

  • The foundation stone was laid by the Prince of Wales in 1906.
  • The central dome is topped by a 16-foot bronze statue of Victory that rotates with the wind.
  • The architecture combines British, Mughal, Venetian, and Egyptian influences.
  • The memorial was funded largely by Indian princely states under British rule.
  • It is maintained by the Ministry of Culture, Government of India.
