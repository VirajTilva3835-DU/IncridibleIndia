About Darjeeling

Nestled in the foothills of the Eastern Himalayas, Darjeeling is one of India’s most famous hill stations, known for its scenic beauty, colonial charm, and aromatic tea gardens. Located in West Bengal, it offers breathtaking views of the snow-capped Kanchenjunga, the world’s third-highest peak.


The town’s rich history, vibrant culture, and pleasant climate attract travelers from around the world. The iconic Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, also known as the Toy Train, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that winds its way through lush hills, offering a nostalgic journey.


Interesting Facts

  • The Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (Toy Train) was built between 1879 and 1881 and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Darjeeling tea is world-famous for its unique aroma and flavor, often called the “Champagne of Teas.”
  • Kanchenjunga, visible from Darjeeling, is the world’s third-highest mountain (8,586 m).
  • The town’s name comes from Tibetan words “Dorje” (thunderbolt) and “Ling” (place) — meaning “Land of the Thunderbolt.”
  • It was once a summer resort for the British elite during colonial times.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Don’t miss the sunrise from Tiger Hill for spectacular Kanchenjunga views.
  • Visit Happy Valley Tea Estate to see tea processing in action.
  • Take a ride on the Darjeeling Toy Train for a nostalgic experience.
  • Other nearby spots: Batasia Loop, Peace Pagoda, and Ghoom Monastery.
  • Carry warm clothes — temperatures can drop suddenly, even in summer.
