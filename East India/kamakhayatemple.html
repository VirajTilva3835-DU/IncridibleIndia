Kamakhya Temple, Assam

"Ancient and powerful Shakti Peeth dedicated to Goddess Kamakhya, located in Guwahati."

About Kamakhya Temple

The Kamakhya Temple, located atop the Nilachal Hill in Guwahati, Assam, is one of the oldest and most revered Shakti Peethas in India. It is dedicated to Goddess Kamakhya, an incarnation of Goddess Parvati. The temple marks the spot where, according to mythology, the yoni (womb) of Goddess Sati fell, symbolizing feminine power and fertility.


The temple was rebuilt in 1565 CE by King Naranarayana of the Koch dynasty. Its unique Nilachal-style architecture, with a hemispherical dome and beehive-like shikhara, adds to its spiritual grandeur. Every year, it hosts the famous Ambubachi Mela, celebrating the goddess’s annual menstruation cycle.


Interesting Facts

  • Kamakhya is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas — believed to be where the goddess’s womb fell.
  • The sanctum does not contain an idol but a natural rock cleft symbolizing the yoni of the goddess.
  • The Ambubachi Mela is known as the “Tantric Fertility Festival of the East.”
  • The temple is a major center of Tantric worship and attracts devotees from across the world.
  • The temple complex consists of 10 other temples dedicated to different forms of the Goddess.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Early morning (5 AM – 7 AM) is the best time to experience peaceful darshan.
  • Dress modestly and avoid visiting during peak festival hours if you dislike crowds.
  • Carry water and wear comfortable shoes — stairs can be steep.
  • Nearby attractions: Umananda Temple, Assam State Zoo, Srimanta Sankardev Kalakshetra, and Brahmaputra River Cruise.
