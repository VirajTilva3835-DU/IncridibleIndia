Chilika Lake, Odisha

"Asia’s largest brackish water lagoon, famous for migratory birds and dolphins."

About Chilika Lake

Chilika Lake, located on the east coast of India in Odisha, is the largest brackish water lagoon in Asia and the second-largest in the world. Spread over 1,100 square kilometers, it extends across the districts of Puri, Khordha, and Ganjam. The lake is a biodiversity hotspot, supporting more than 225 species of fish and numerous migratory birds.


Declared a Ramsar Wetland of International Importance in 1981, Chilika Lake attracts bird watchers, nature lovers, and eco-tourists. It is also home to the endangered Irrawaddy dolphins, which can be spotted near Satapada.


Interesting Facts

  • Chilika Lake connects to the Bay of Bengal through a narrow sea mouth near Satapada.
  • It hosts over 160 species of migratory birds during winter, coming from as far as Siberia and the Caspian region.
  • The Irrawaddy dolphins here are among the few populations found outside Southeast Asia.
  • The lake’s ecosystem changes from freshwater to saline depending on monsoon and sea tides.
  • It supports over 150,000 fisherfolk living in nearby villages.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Take a dolphin-watching boat ride at Satapada early in the morning.
  • Don’t miss Kalijai Temple located on a small island in the lake.
  • Carry binoculars for bird watching, especially during winter.
  • Nearby attractions: Barkul Viewpoint, Nalabana Bird Sanctuary, Rambha Bay, and Puri Beach.
