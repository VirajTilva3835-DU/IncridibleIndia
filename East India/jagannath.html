Jagannath Temple, Puri (Odisha)

"One of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites — renowned for its grand Rath Yatra festival."

About the Jagannath Temple

The Jagannath Temple in Puri, Odisha, is one of the most revered Hindu temples in India and a part of the sacred Char Dham pilgrimage. Dedicated to Lord Jagannath (a form of Lord Vishnu), the temple is famous for its annual Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival), during which the deities are taken out in grand processions.


Built in the 12th century by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva of the Eastern Ganga dynasty, the temple is an architectural masterpiece reflecting Kalinga style. Non-Hindus are not allowed inside the main temple complex, but they can view it from the nearby Raghunandan Library rooftop.


Interesting Facts

  • The temple’s flag always flutters in the opposite direction of the wind.
  • No shadow of the main dome is visible at any time of the day.
  • The Rath Yatra draws over a million devotees from all over the world each year.
  • The temple kitchen is considered the largest in the world, cooking for over 50,000 people daily.
  • Even the sea waves seem to calm down as they approach the temple area — a mystery still unsolved.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Non-Hindus can view the temple from the Raghunandan Library viewpoint.
  • Footwear, mobile phones, and leather items are not allowed inside.
  • Book accommodation early during Rath Yatra season (June–July).
  • Nearby attractions: Puri Beach, Konark Sun Temple, Chilika Lake, and Gundicha Temple.
