Kaziranga National Park, Assam

"UNESCO World Heritage Site known for the one-horned rhinoceros and diverse wildlife."

About Kaziranga National Park

Located in the Indian state of Assam, Kaziranga National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of India’s most celebrated wildlife reserves. It is globally famous for housing the largest population of the one-horned rhinoceros, an endangered species found only in India and Nepal.


Spread across the floodplains of the Brahmaputra River, Kaziranga offers a diverse ecosystem of grasslands, wetlands, and forests. The park is also home to elephants, tigers, wild water buffaloes, swamp deer, and over 500 species of birds, making it a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers.


Interesting Facts

  • Kaziranga hosts about two-thirds of the world's one-horned rhinoceros population.
  • The park was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1985.
  • It is one of India’s few parks where all members of the “Big Five” — rhino, tiger, elephant, buffalo, and deer — coexist.
  • The park is divided into four tourism zones: Kohora, Bagori, Agaratoli, and Burapahar.
  • Declared a Tiger Reserve in 2006 due to its growing tiger population density.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Book safaris in advance — both jeep and elephant safaris are popular.
  • Carry binoculars and a zoom camera for birdwatching.
  • Wear earthy-colored clothes and avoid bright colors during safaris.
  • Visit nearby attractions like Kakochang Waterfalls, Hollongapar Gibbon Sanctuary, and Majuli Island.
  • Follow forest department rules — do not feed or disturb wildlife.
