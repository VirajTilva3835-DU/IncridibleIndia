Aizawl, Mizoram

"Peaceful hill city known for its panoramic views and Mizo culture."

About Aizawl

Aizawl, the capital city of Mizoram, is a peaceful hill station perched at about 1,132 meters above sea level. It offers mesmerizing panoramic views of lush hills and valleys. The city is a blend of modern development and traditional Mizo culture, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking tranquility and cultural richness.


Established in the late 19th century by the British, Aizawl is now the center of administration and culture in Mizoram. Visitors can explore vibrant markets, traditional handicrafts, and taste authentic Mizo cuisine. The friendly locals and serene environment make it a must-visit destination in Northeast India.


Interesting Facts

  • Aizawl is one of the cleanest and most peaceful cities in India.
  • It offers breathtaking views of the Tlawng and Tuirial river valleys.
  • The city is home to the Mizoram State Museum showcasing Mizo heritage.
  • Handloom and handicraft products from Aizawl are famous across Northeast India.
  • The Mizo people celebrate unique festivals like Chapchar Kut with dance and music.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly in rural areas.
  • Try traditional dishes like Bai and Bamboo shoot curry.
  • Local taxis are the main mode of transport within the city.
  • Nearby attractions: Durtlang Hills, Reiek Tlang, Mizoram State Museum, and Vantawng Falls.
