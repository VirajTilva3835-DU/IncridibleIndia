Loktak Lake, Manipur

"Freshwater lake with floating islands (phumdis), home to Keibul Lamjao National Park."

About Loktak Lake

Located near Moirang in Manipur, Loktak Lake is the largest freshwater lake in Northeast India and one of the most unique ecosystems in the world. What makes this lake special are the phumdis — floating islands made of vegetation, soil, and organic matter.


The lake is also home to the Keibul Lamjao National Park, the only floating national park in the world, which shelters the endangered Sangai deer (also known as the dancing deer). Loktak Lake serves as a vital lifeline for local communities, providing water, fish, and livelihoods to thousands of people living around it.


Interesting Facts

  • Loktak is the largest freshwater lake in Northeast India.
  • The only floating national park in the world — Keibul Lamjao — is located here.
  • Home to the endangered Sangai deer, Manipur’s state animal.
  • Phumdis (floating islands) cover almost half of the lake’s surface.
  • Loktak Hydro Project helps generate electricity for the region.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Take a boat ride to see the floating phumdis up close.
  • Visit Keibul Lamjao National Park early morning for the best chance to spot Sangai deer.
  • Carry binoculars — the lake attracts many migratory birds.
  • Nearby attractions: INA Museum, Sendra Island, and Imphal City.
