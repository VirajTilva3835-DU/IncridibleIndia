Konark Sun Temple, Odisha

"Magnificent 13th-century chariot-shaped temple dedicated to the Sun God, Surya."

About the Konark Sun Temple

The Konark Sun Temple, located in Konark, Odisha, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most spectacular monuments in India. Built in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga dynasty, the temple is designed in the form of a colossal chariot dedicated to the Sun God, Surya.


The temple once stood at the edge of the sea and served as a navigational landmark for sailors, who referred to it as the "Black Pagoda" due to its dark hue. Its intricate carvings, detailed sculptures, and massive stone wheels symbolize time and celestial movement.


Interesting Facts

  • The temple is shaped like a gigantic chariot with 24 intricately carved stone wheels pulled by seven horses.
  • Each wheel of the temple can function as a sundial, capable of telling time with remarkable precision.
  • It took over 12 years and thousands of sculptors to complete the monument.
  • The temple was designed so that the first rays of the rising sun would strike the main sanctum.
  • It is featured on the back of the ₹10 Indian currency note.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Best time to visit is during sunrise or sunset when the temple glows with golden light.
  • Carry a hat and water, as the area gets quite sunny during the day.
  • The Konark Dance Festival held every December showcases India’s classical dances.
  • Nearby attractions: Chandrabhaga Beach, Ramachandi Temple, and Pipili village for applique art.
