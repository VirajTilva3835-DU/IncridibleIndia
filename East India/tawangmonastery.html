Tawang Monastery, Arunachal Pradesh

"Largest monastery in India, offering breathtaking views of the Himalayas."

About Tawang Monastery

Nestled at an altitude of 10,000 feet in the Tawang Valley of Arunachal Pradesh, Tawang Monastery is the largest monastery in India and the second-largest in the world after the Potala Palace in Lhasa, Tibet. It is a sacred center of Mahayana Buddhism and a vital cultural and spiritual hub for the Monpa tribe.


Established in the 17th century (1681) by Merak Lama Lodre Gyatso, the monastery is home to nearly 500 monks and features a magnificent 18-foot golden Buddha statue. Surrounded by snow-capped peaks, its hilltop location offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Eastern Himalayas.


Interesting Facts

  • It’s the largest Buddhist monastery in India and second-largest in the world.
  • The monastery complex covers an area of around 140 square meters.
  • Houses ancient manuscripts, scriptures, and a library with rare Buddhist texts.
  • The annual Torgya Festival is celebrated with vibrant monastic dances and rituals.
  • It belongs to the Gelugpa sect of Tibetan Buddhism, like the Dalai Lama.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Carry warm clothes — temperatures can drop below freezing.
  • Foreign tourists need an Inner Line Permit (ILP) to visit Arunachal Pradesh.
  • Attend the Torgya Festival for a glimpse of monastic culture.
  • Nearby attractions: Sela Pass, Nuranang Falls, Pankang Teng Tso Lake.
