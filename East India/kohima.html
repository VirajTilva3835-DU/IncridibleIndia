Kohima, Nagaland

"Historic town known for World War II cemetery and vibrant tribal festivals."

About Kohima

Kohima, the capital city of Nagaland, is a place rich in history and culture. Known for its significant role in World War II, the city houses the famous Kohima War Cemetery, which commemorates the soldiers who laid down their lives during the Battle of Kohima in 1944.


Surrounded by rolling hills and lush greenery, Kohima offers visitors a perfect blend of historical significance and cultural vibrancy. The city is also the venue for the annual Hornbill Festival, showcasing the diverse tribal traditions, music, and dances of Nagaland.


Interesting Facts

  • Kohima was one of the key battle sites during World War II in the Asia-Pacific region.
  • The Kohima War Cemetery is maintained by the Commonwealth War Graves Commission.
  • The Hornbill Festival attracts thousands of visitors every December.
  • Kohima is home to 16 major tribes of Nagaland, each with its own unique culture and language.
  • The city offers panoramic views of the Japfu Peak and Dzukou Valley.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Attend the Hornbill Festival (December 1–10) for the best cultural experience.
  • Visit the Kohima War Cemetery and Naga Heritage Village (Kisama).
  • Respect local customs and avoid photographing people without permission.
  • Nearby attractions: Dzukou Valley, Japfu Peak, and Khonoma Village.
