About Shillong

Nestled amidst rolling hills and misty valleys, Shillong is the charming capital of Meghalaya, often referred to as the “Scotland of the East.” Known for its pleasant climate, pine forests, and cascading waterfalls, it offers a refreshing escape for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.


The city reflects a unique blend of British-era charm and modern vibrancy. Its rich music culture and warm hospitality make Shillong one of India’s most beloved hill stations. From the mesmerizing Elephant Falls to panoramic views at Shillong Peak, every corner has something special to offer.


Interesting Facts

  • Shillong is known as the Music Capital of India — home to many rock and jazz bands.
  • It served as the capital of Assam before Meghalaya became a separate state in 1972.
  • The name "Shillong" comes from Lei Shyllong, a deity worshipped at the Shillong Peak.
  • It houses India’s first and only golf course built at such a high altitude.
  • It receives some of the highest rainfall in India due to its proximity to Cherrapunji and Mawsynram.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Carry light woolens — Shillong weather is cool throughout the year.
  • Don’t miss the Shillong Peak and Elephant Falls for panoramic views.
  • Try local Khasi cuisine at Police Bazaar or Laitumkhrah.
  • Nearby attractions: Cherrapunji, Umiam Lake, Mawsynram, and Living Root Bridges.
