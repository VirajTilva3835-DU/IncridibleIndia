Sundarbans, West Bengal

"Largest mangrove forest and tiger reserve, home to the famous Royal Bengal Tiger."

About Sundarbans

The Sundarbans is the world’s largest mangrove forest, spanning India and Bangladesh, located at the delta formed by the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers. It is famous for being the natural habitat of the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger.


The Indian portion of the Sundarbans covers an area of around 4,200 sq km and is home to diverse flora and fauna, including saltwater crocodiles, spotted deer, Gangetic dolphins, and hundreds of bird species. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1987, it is a unique blend of biodiversity and natural beauty.


Interesting Facts

  • The name “Sundarbans” means “Beautiful Forest” — derived from the Sundari trees found here.
  • It is the only mangrove forest in the world where tigers are found.
  • Home to more than 400 tigers and 250 species of birds.
  • The tigers here are excellent swimmers and have adapted to the saline environment.
  • Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and Ramsar Wetland.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Book a licensed boat safari for safety and authorized entry.
  • Carry binoculars and insect repellent.
  • Best to stay overnight at Sajnekhali or Pakhiralay for full experience.
  • Nearby attractions include Henry Island, Bakkhali Beach, and Jambudwip Island.
