India Gate, Delhi

"A grand war memorial honoring the brave soldiers of India, standing tall in the heart of the capital."

About the India Gate

The India Gate is a majestic war memorial located on the ceremonial boulevard of Rajpath in New Delhi. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, it was built in 1931 to honor the 84,000 soldiers of the British Indian Army who lost their lives during World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War.


The monument stands at a height of 42 meters (138 feet) and bears the names of over 13,000 soldiers inscribed on its surface. Beneath the arch lies the Amar Jawan Jyoti (Flame of the Immortal Soldier), added after India’s independence to commemorate the soldiers who died in the 1971 Indo-Pak war.


Interesting Facts

  • India Gate was designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, the chief architect of New Delhi.
  • The Amar Jawan Jyoti flame burned continuously from 1972 to 2022.
  • It resembles the Arc de Triomphe in Paris and the Arch of Constantine in Rome.
  • The lawns surrounding India Gate are a popular evening spot for locals and tourists.
  • It is beautifully illuminated at night with the tricolor lights of the Indian flag.
