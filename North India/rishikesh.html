Rishikesh, Uttarakhand

"The Yoga Capital of the World — where spirituality meets adventure."

About Rishikesh

Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas, Rishikesh is a sacred town in Uttarakhand, known as the Yoga Capital of the World. Situated on the banks of the River Ganges, it attracts spiritual seekers, adventure enthusiasts, and peace lovers alike.


The town is renowned for its Ganga Aarti at Triveni Ghat, ashrams offering yoga and meditation courses, and adventure sports like river rafting and bungee jumping. It is also the gateway to the Char Dham Yatra — Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath.


Interesting Facts

  • Rishikesh became world-famous after The Beatles visited the Maharishi Mahesh Yogi Ashram in 1968.
  • The town is completely vegetarian and alcohol-free by law.
  • The Lakshman Jhula and Ram Jhula are iconic suspension bridges across the Ganges.
  • It hosts the International Yoga Festival every year in March.
  • It is one of the best places in India for white-water rafting.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Attend the evening Ganga Aarti at Triveni Ghat for a divine experience.
  • Try river rafting or cliff jumping for adventure lovers.
  • Stay at an ashram to experience authentic yoga and meditation.
  • Nearby attractions: Haridwar, Neelkanth Mahadev Temple, Beatles Ashram, and Rajaji National Park.
