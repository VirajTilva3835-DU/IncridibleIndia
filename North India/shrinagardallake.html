About Srinagar & Dal Lake

Srinagar, the summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir, is a paradise on earth known for its snow-capped peaks, colorful gardens, and tranquil lakes. The centerpiece of the city is the iconic Dal Lake, famous for its shimmering waters and floating gardens.


The serene shikara rides, traditional houseboats, and the backdrop of the Zabarwan Range make Srinagar one of India’s most romantic and scenic destinations. The Mughal gardens, wooden bridges, and bustling floating markets add to its timeless charm.


Interesting Facts

  • Dal Lake is often called the “Jewel in the crown of Kashmir”.
  • The lake covers around 18 square kilometers and features floating gardens known as “Rad”.
  • Srinagar’s houseboats date back to the British colonial era when they weren’t allowed to own land.
  • The famous Mughal gardens — Shalimar Bagh and Nishat Bagh — lie on the banks of Dal Lake.
  • Dal Lake freezes completely during severe winters.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Take a shikara ride at sunrise or sunset for breathtaking views.
  • Book a stay in a traditional houseboat for a unique experience.
  • Don’t miss Shalimar Bagh, Nishat Bagh, and Chashme Shahi.
  • Winter months offer the chance to see a partially frozen Dal Lake.
  • Try local Kashmiri cuisine like Rogan Josh and Kahwa tea.
