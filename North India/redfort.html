Red Fort, Delhi

"A Mughal masterpiece and symbol of India’s independence and heritage."

About the Red Fort

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a grand Mughal-era fort complex located in the heart of Delhi. Constructed by Emperor Shah Jahan between 1638 and 1648, it served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for over 200 years. Built from red sandstone, the fort stands as a timeless symbol of India’s architectural brilliance and cultural legacy.


Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2007, the Red Fort is not only a marvel of Mughal architecture but also a national symbol of India’s freedom. Every year, on 15th August, the Prime Minister hoists the national flag from its ramparts to celebrate India’s Independence Day.


Interesting Facts

  • The Red Fort was originally known as Qila-e-Mubarak, meaning “The Blessed Fort”.
  • It took around 10 years to complete, from 1638 to 1648.
  • The fort’s red sandstone walls stretch for over 2.5 kilometers.
  • It houses famous structures like Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, and the Rang Mahal.
  • The fort’s architecture reflects a blend of Persian, Timurid, and Indian styles.
