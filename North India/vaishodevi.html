Vaishno Devi, Jammu and Kashmir

"Famous Hindu pilgrimage site located in the Trikuta Mountains near Katra."

About Vaishno Devi

Vaishno Devi is one of the most revered Hindu pilgrimage destinations in India, dedicated to Goddess Vaishnavi. The temple is located in the Trikuta Mountains near Katra in Jammu and Kashmir and attracts millions of devotees every year. The shrine is believed to fulfill the wishes of those who visit with a pure heart and deep devotion.


The holy cave of Vaishno Devi houses the three natural rock formations known as the Pindis, symbolizing Goddess Kali, Goddess Saraswati, and Goddess Lakshmi. Pilgrims undertake a trek of about 13 kilometers from the base camp at Katra to reach the shrine, passing through scenic hills and devotional chants.


Interesting Facts

  • The Vaishno Devi temple is located at an altitude of approximately 5,200 feet above sea level.
  • Over 1 crore (10 million) devotees visit the shrine every year, making it one of India’s most visited religious sites.
  • The holy cave is believed to be over a million years old.
  • Helicopter services and ponies are available for pilgrims unable to trek the entire distance.
  • The shrine is managed by the Shri Mata Vaishno Devi Shrine Board (SMVDSB).

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Start your trek early morning to avoid rush and heat.
  • Helicopter and battery car facilities are available up to Sanjichhat and Ardhkuwari.
  • Carry light woollens throughout the year due to cool mountain air.
  • Nearby attractions include Bhairon Temple, Patnitop, and Shiv Khori Cave Temple.
