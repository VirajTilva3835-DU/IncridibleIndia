Dharamshala & McLeod Ganj, Himachal Pradesh

"Peaceful towns surrounded by mountains, home to the Dalai Lama and Tibetan culture."

About Dharamshala & McLeod Ganj

Dharamshala, nestled in the Kangra Valley, is one of Himachal Pradesh’s most serene hill towns. It serves as the administrative headquarters of the Kangra district and is surrounded by cedar forests, snowy peaks, and Buddhist monasteries.


Just above Dharamshala lies McLeod Ganj — also known as “Little Lhasa” — famous as the residence of His Holiness the 14th Dalai Lama and the center of Tibetan culture in India. The area is known for its peaceful atmosphere, monasteries, cafes, and treks through the Dhauladhar mountains.


Interesting Facts

  • Dharamshala is the headquarters of the Tibetan government-in-exile.
  • The Dalai Lama settled here after fleeing Tibet in 1959.
  • McLeod Ganj is named after Sir Donald McLeod, a British lieutenant governor of Punjab.
  • The town hosts the annual Tibetan Opera Festival at the Tsuglagkhang Complex.
  • It’s a major destination for yoga, meditation, and volunteer tourism.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Visit the Dalai Lama Temple Complex for peace and prayer sessions.
  • Enjoy the Triund Trek for breathtaking mountain views.
  • Try Tibetan momos and thukpa in local cafes.
  • Explore Bhagsunag Waterfall and St. John in the Wilderness Church.
  • Carry light woollens even in summer; evenings can be cool.
