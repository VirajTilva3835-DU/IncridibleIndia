Varanasi Ghats, Varanasi (Uttar Pradesh)

"The spiritual heart of India — where faith, life, and eternity meet on the banks of the Ganga."

About Varanasi Ghats

The Ghats of Varanasi, located on the sacred banks of the River Ganga, are the lifeline of the city and hold immense spiritual, cultural, and historical significance. Known as the oldest living city in the world, Varanasi is revered by millions of devotees who come to take a holy dip, perform rituals, and witness the divine Ganga Aarti.


The city is home to over 80 ghats, each with a unique story and purpose — from religious ceremonies to cremation rituals. The most famous among them are Dashashwamedh Ghat, Manikarnika Ghat, and Assi Ghat. The mesmerizing evening aarti, the chanting of mantras, and the flickering lamps on the water create an unforgettable spiritual experience.


Interesting Facts

  • Varanasi is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world.
  • There are more than 80 ghats along the riverbank, each serving different purposes.
  • The Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat is performed daily by priests with synchronized chants and lamps.
  • Manikarnika Ghat is one of the most sacred cremation sites for Hindus.
  • Many ghats were built or renovated by Maratha rulers between the 17th and 19th centuries.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Attend the Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat (evening) or Assi Ghat (morning).
  • Take a sunrise boat ride on the Ganga for the best views.
  • Wear modest clothing and respect local customs.
  • Nearby attractions: Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Sarnath, Ramnagar Fort, and Banaras Hindu University.
