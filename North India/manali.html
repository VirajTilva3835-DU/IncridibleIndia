Manali, Himachal Pradesh

"Adventure hub surrounded by mountains and valleys, famous for Solang and Rohtang."

About Manali

Manali is a picturesque hill station located in the Kullu Valley of Himachal Pradesh. Surrounded by snow-capped mountains, lush valleys, and river streams, it is a paradise for adventure seekers, honeymooners, and nature lovers alike.


The town offers year-round attractions, from skiing and paragliding in winter to trekking and rafting in summer. Nearby destinations like Solang Valley and Rohtang Pass make it one of India’s most popular travel spots.


Interesting Facts

  • Manali is named after the sage Manu, believed to be the creator of mankind in Hindu mythology.
  • It is the gateway to the Lahaul and Spiti Valley via the Rohtang Pass.
  • Adventure sports like skiing, paragliding, rafting, and zorbing are major tourist attractions.
  • The nearby Atal Tunnel is the world’s longest highway tunnel above 10,000 feet.
  • Manali’s Hidimba Devi Temple, built in 1553, is a unique wooden temple dedicated to Hidimba from the Mahabharata.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Carry warm clothes — temperatures can drop suddenly even in summer.
  • Book Rohtang Pass permits in advance (limited daily entry).
  • Try adventure sports at Solang Valley like paragliding and zorbing.
  • Visit Old Manali for cafes, local crafts, and relaxed vibes.
  • Explore nearby Kullu, Kasol, and Manikaran for extended trips.
