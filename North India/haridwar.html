Haridwar, Uttarakhand

"The Gateway to the Gods — where the sacred Ganges leaves the Himalayas."

About Haridwar

Haridwar, meaning "Gateway to the Gods," is one of the seven holiest cities in India. Located at the foothills of the Himalayas in Uttarakhand, it marks the point where the sacred River Ganges leaves the mountains and enters the plains.


The city is world-renowned for the Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri, a mesmerizing spiritual ceremony held every evening. Pilgrims from across India visit to take a holy dip in the Ganges, believed to cleanse sins and purify the soul. Haridwar also serves as one of the sites of the Kumbh Mela, held every 12 years — one of the largest human gatherings on Earth.


Interesting Facts

  • Haridwar is one of the four places where drops of the elixir of immortality (Amrit) fell during the Samudra Manthan.
  • The famous Kumbh Mela attracts millions of devotees every 12 years.
  • Har Ki Pauri is believed to have Lord Vishnu’s footprint on a stone wall.
  • The city bans non-vegetarian food and alcohol as part of its sacred status.
  • It is the gateway to the Char Dham Yatra pilgrimage circuit.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Attend the evening Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri for a divine experience.
  • Take a dip in the holy Ganga — especially at sunrise for peace and purity.
  • Avoid visiting during peak Kumbh Mela if you prefer smaller crowds.
  • Nearby attractions: Rishikesh, Mansa Devi Temple, Chandi Devi Temple, and Rajaji National Park.
