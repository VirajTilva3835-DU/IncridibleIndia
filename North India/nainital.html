About Nainital

Nainital, often called the "Lake District of India," is a charming hill station nestled in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand. Surrounded by lush green hills and centered around the shimmering Naini Lake, it is one of India’s most visited summer destinations. The cool climate, scenic beauty, and colonial architecture make it a perfect getaway for nature lovers and families.


Established by the British in the 19th century, Nainital still retains its colonial charm through heritage buildings and old churches. The bustling Mall Road, peaceful Naina Devi Temple, and panoramic viewpoints like Snow View and Tiffin Top make Nainital a perfect mix of relaxation and exploration.


Interesting Facts

  • The town is named after the sacred Naini Lake, believed to be one of the 64 Shakti Peethas in India.
  • Nainital was the summer capital of the United Provinces during British rule.
  • The lake is pear-shaped and surrounded by seven hills known as the Sapta-Shring.
  • The highest point in Nainital is Naina Peak (2,615 m), offering stunning views of the Himalayas.
  • It’s one of the best spots in North India for boating, cable car rides, and short treks.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Boating at Naini Lake is a must-do — early morning or sunset offers the best experience.
  • Carry warm clothes, especially during evenings and winters.
  • Shop for woollens, candles, and local handicrafts at Mall Road and Tibetan Market.
  • Nearby attractions: Bhimtal, Sattal, Naukuchiatal, and Jim Corbett National Park.
