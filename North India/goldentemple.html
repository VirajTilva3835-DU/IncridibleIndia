Golden Temple, Amritsar (Punjab)

"The holiest shrine of Sikhism — a symbol of equality, peace, and divine beauty."

About the Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib or Darbar Sahib, is the most sacred shrine of Sikhism located in Amritsar, Punjab. The temple’s gleaming golden façade and serene water tank, known as the Amrit Sarovar, create a breathtaking sight that attracts millions of visitors from across the globe.


Originally built in 1581 by Guru Arjan Dev Ji, the temple was later renovated with marble and gold by Maharaja Ranjit Singh in the early 19th century. It stands as a symbol of equality, humility, and spirituality, welcoming people of all faiths.


Interesting Facts

  • The temple’s upper floors are covered in over 750 kg of pure gold.
  • The foundation stone was laid by a Muslim saint, Hazrat Mian Mir, emphasizing communal harmony.
  • The temple is surrounded by the sacred Amrit Sarovar, from which the city of Amritsar gets its name.
  • The world’s largest free community kitchen (Langar) serves over 100,000 meals daily.
  • It has four entrances symbolizing openness to people from all directions and walks of life.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Remove shoes and cover your head before entering the complex.
  • Try the Langar (free meal) for an authentic Sikh experience.
  • The temple is stunningly illuminated at night — don’t miss the reflection in the water.
  • Nearby attractions: Jallianwala Bagh, Partition Museum, and Wagah Border.
