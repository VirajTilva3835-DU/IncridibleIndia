Ayodhya Ram Mandir, Uttar Pradesh

"The grand temple dedicated to Lord Rama — symbol of devotion and unity in Ayodhya."

About the Ayodhya Ram Mandir

The Ayodhya Ram Mandir is a magnificent Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Rama, located in Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh. Built at the sacred site believed to be the birthplace of Lord Rama, the temple stands as a monumental symbol of faith, unity, and cultural heritage.


The foundation stone was laid by Prime Minister Narendra Modi on August 5, 2020, and the temple’s grand inauguration took place on January 22, 2024. The architecture blends ancient Indian temple design with modern construction techniques, representing the pinnacle of devotion and artistry.


Interesting Facts

  • The temple is built entirely using Bansi Paharpur pink sandstone without the use of steel or iron.
  • The main sanctum (Garbha Griha) houses the idol of Ram Lalla (child form of Lord Rama).
  • The temple’s total height is approximately 161 feet with three floors.
  • More than 600 sculptors, artisans, and engineers have worked on the project.
  • The temple complex spans over 70 acres including gardens, museums, and pilgrim facilities.
