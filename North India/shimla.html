Shimla, Himachal Pradesh

"Hill station known for colonial charm, Mall Road, and snowy mountain views."

About Shimla

Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh, is one of India’s most famous hill stations. Known as the “Queen of Hills,” it was the summer capital of British India and still retains its colonial-era charm with scenic mountain views, old buildings, and vibrant streets.


Surrounded by snow-capped peaks and dense deodar forests, Shimla offers a blend of heritage, adventure, and natural beauty. Its bustling Mall Road, historic Christ Church, and panoramic viewpoints make it a must-visit destination for travelers across the world.


Interesting Facts

  • Shimla was declared the summer capital of British India in 1864.
  • The famous Kalka–Shimla toy train is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • It is built across seven hills, offering different viewpoints and attractions on each.
  • The city hosts the annual Ice Skating Carnival — India’s only natural ice skating rink.
  • Shimla was named after the goddess Shyamala Devi, an incarnation of Goddess Kali.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Best time to visit for snow: late December to early February.
  • Wear warm clothing, especially at night or during snowfall.
  • Enjoy a toy train ride from Kalka to Shimla for breathtaking views.
  • Explore nearby places like Kufri, Chail, and Solan.
  • Try local Himachali cuisine and shop for woolens at Mall Road.
