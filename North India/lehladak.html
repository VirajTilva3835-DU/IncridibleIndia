About Leh–Ladakh

Leh–Ladakh is one of India’s most stunning and adventurous destinations, located in the northernmost region of Jammu and Kashmir. Known for its high-altitude desert, rugged mountains, crystal-clear lakes, and ancient monasteries, it’s a paradise for travelers seeking tranquility and thrill alike.


The region is home to breathtaking attractions such as Pangong Lake, Nubra Valley, Magnetic Hill, and Thiksey Monastery. Adventure enthusiasts can enjoy biking, trekking, rafting, and camping amidst surreal landscapes. Ladakh’s Buddhist culture, prayer flags, and festivals make it an unforgettable experience.


Interesting Facts

  • Leh is situated at an altitude of about 11,500 feet above sea level.
  • Pangong Lake freezes completely during winter, despite being a saline water lake.
  • The famous Magnetic Hill gives an illusion of vehicles moving uphill on their own.
  • Ladakh is often called “The Land of High Passes.”
  • It has some of the highest motorable roads in the world — Khardung La and Chang La.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Acclimatize for a day or two before starting any adventure activities.
  • Carry warm clothes, sunscreen, and basic medicines — weather changes rapidly.
  • ATMs are limited, so carry enough cash.
  • Popular nearby attractions: Pangong Lake, Nubra Valley, Shanti Stupa, Magnetic Hill, and Lamayuru Monastery.
