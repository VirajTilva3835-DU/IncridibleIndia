Rock Garden, Chandigarh

"A unique sculpture garden created from industrial and household waste materials."

About the Rock Garden

The Rock Garden of Chandigarh is an extraordinary open-air exhibition of sculptures made from industrial and household waste. It was created by Nek Chand, a government official who secretly built this art marvel in his spare time during the 1950s.


The garden covers an area of over 40 acres and features thousands of sculptures made from discarded materials like broken bangles, tiles, bottles, ceramics, and electrical waste. It is a symbol of creativity, sustainability, and the power of imagination.


Interesting Facts

  • The garden was built secretly by Nek Chand for nearly 18 years before being discovered in 1975.
  • It uses completely recycled materials and discarded items to form stunning art installations.
  • There are over 5,000 sculptures, including human figures, animals, and abstract designs.
  • The Rock Garden is divided into three phases, each featuring waterfalls, courtyards, and mosaic art.
  • It attracts more than a million visitors annually from around the world.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Carry a camera — the garden is full of photogenic art and creative sculptures.
  • Wear comfortable shoes, as walking paths are uneven in some areas.
  • Don’t miss the nearby Sukhna Lake, Rose Garden, and Capitol Complex.
  • Visit during the day to enjoy natural light on the art installations.
  • Snacks and refreshments are available near the entrance area.
