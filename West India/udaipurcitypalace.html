Udaipur City Palace, Udaipur (Rajasthan)

"A magnificent royal residence overlooking Lake Pichola — a blend of Rajasthani and Mughal architecture."

About the Udaipur City Palace

The City Palace of Udaipur is one of the largest royal complexes in Rajasthan, majestically overlooking Lake Pichola. Built by Maharana Udai Singh II in 1559, it served as the principal seat of the Mewar dynasty. Over the centuries, his successors continued expanding the palace, blending Rajasthani, Mughal, European, and Chinese architectural styles.


The palace complex is a grand fusion of marble and granite structures, featuring courtyards, terraces, gardens, and lavish rooms adorned with intricate mirror work, murals, and colored glass. From its balconies and towers, visitors can enjoy breathtaking panoramic views of the city, Lake Pichola, and surrounding Aravalli Hills.


Interesting Facts

  • Construction began in 1559 and took over 400 years to complete with contributions from several rulers.
  • The palace complex consists of 11 smaller palaces built at different times.
  • City Palace has secret tunnels connecting it to other royal residences and escape routes.
  • The architecture reflects a perfect blend of Mughal and Rajasthani craftsmanship.
  • It has featured in famous films like “Octopussy” and “Guide.”
