Ranthambore National Park, Rajasthan

"Famous tiger reserve offering thrilling jungle safaris amid historic ruins and lakes."

About Ranthambore National Park

Ranthambore National Park is one of the most renowned wildlife reserves in India, located near Sawai Madhopur in Rajasthan. Once the royal hunting grounds of the Maharajas of Jaipur, it is now a major conservation area and a key habitat for the majestic Bengal tiger.


The park spreads over 392 square kilometers and includes grasslands, lakes, rocky hills, and dense forests. Apart from tigers, it shelters leopards, sloth bears, crocodiles, and over 300 species of birds — making it a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers.


Interesting Facts

  • Ranthambore is named after the historic Ranthambore Fort located inside the park.
  • It was declared a wildlife sanctuary in 1957 and became a national park in 1980.
  • It is one of the best places in India to spot tigers in the wild.
  • The park’s lakes — Padam Talao, Malik Talao, and Rajbagh Talao — attract many animals and birds.
  • Ranthambore is part of India’s Project Tiger conservation initiative.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Book your safari online in advance — limited slots per day.
  • Carry binoculars and a camera with good zoom for wildlife spotting.
  • Wear earthy or neutral colors to blend with surroundings.
  • Nearby attractions: Ranthambore Fort, Surwal Lake, Kachida Valley, and Rajbagh Ruins.
