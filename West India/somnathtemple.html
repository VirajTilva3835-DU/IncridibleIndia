Somnath Temple, Gujarat

"One of the twelve Jyotirlinga shrines of Lord Shiva, located on the Arabian coast."

About Somnath Temple

Somnath Temple is one of the twelve Jyotirlinga shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in Prabhas Patan near Veraval, Gujarat. It stands majestically on the Arabian Sea coast and holds immense historical and spiritual significance for Hindus.


The temple has been destroyed and rebuilt several times throughout history — by invaders and rulers alike — symbolizing the enduring strength of faith. The present structure was reconstructed in 1951 under the vision of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, showcasing Chalukya-style architecture.


Interesting Facts

  • Somnath is the first of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva across India.
  • The temple’s name means “Protector of the Moon God.”
  • It has been rebuilt 17 times after repeated invasions.
  • The current temple was inaugurated by Dr. Rajendra Prasad, the first President of India.
  • There is no landmass between Somnath and Antarctica in a straight line to the south — a unique geographical fact.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Attend the evening Aarti and Sound & Light Show for an unforgettable experience.
  • Footwear and mobile phones are not allowed inside the temple premises.
  • Nearby attractions include Bhalka Tirth, Triveni Sangam, and Prabhas Patan Museum.
  • Visit during Mahashivratri for vibrant festivities and spiritual energy.
