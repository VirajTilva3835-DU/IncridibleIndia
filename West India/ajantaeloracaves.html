Ajanta & Ellora Caves, Aurangabad (Maharashtra)

"Ancient rock-cut caves showcasing Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain temples and sculptures."

About Ajanta & Ellora Caves

The Ajanta and Ellora Caves, located near Aurangabad in Maharashtra, are among the most remarkable examples of ancient rock-cut architecture in India. These UNESCO World Heritage Sites showcase exquisite craftsmanship and spiritual devotion, representing Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain faiths.


The Ajanta Caves date back to the 2nd century BCE and are celebrated for their magnificent murals and paintings that depict the life of Lord Buddha and stories from the Jataka tales. The Ellora Caves, constructed between the 6th and 10th centuries CE, are famous for the awe-inspiring Kailasa Temple, carved entirely out of a single rock and dedicated to Lord Shiva.


Interesting Facts

  • The Ajanta Caves were rediscovered in 1819 by a British officer during a hunting expedition.
  • The Kailasa Temple at Ellora is believed to have been carved from top to bottom using chisels and hammers.
  • Ajanta’s paintings are created using natural pigments that have survived over 2,000 years.
  • Ellora represents religious harmony — it houses Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain monuments in one complex.
  • Over 100 caves exist in total, though only a portion is open to the public.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Carry a flashlight — some caves have dim lighting inside.
  • Photography is allowed in most areas but flash is prohibited near murals.
  • Wear comfortable shoes; some caves require a short hike.
  • Nearby attractions: Daulatabad Fort, Bibi Ka Maqbara, and Grishneshwar Jyotirlinga Temple.
