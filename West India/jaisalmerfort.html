Jaisalmer Fort, Jaisalmer (Rajasthan)

"The golden fortress of the Thar Desert — a living fort filled with palaces and temples."

About the Jaisalmer Fort

The Jaisalmer Fort, also known as Sonar Quila or the “Golden Fort,” is one of the largest fully preserved fortified cities in the world. Located in the heart of the Thar Desert, it was built in 1156 AD by Rawal Jaisal, a Bhati Rajput ruler. The fort’s golden sandstone walls glow brilliantly under sunlight, giving Jaisalmer its nickname — “The Golden City.”


Unlike most forts, Jaisalmer Fort is a living fort, home to thousands of residents and bustling with shops, temples, restaurants, and guesthouses. Its winding lanes and intricate architecture make it a remarkable blend of heritage and daily life.


Interesting Facts

  • Jaisalmer Fort is one of the few “living forts” in the world with over 4,000 residents inside.
  • It was built in 1156 AD by Rawal Jaisal, the founder of Jaisalmer city.
  • The fort is made of yellow sandstone, giving it a golden hue during the day.
  • It houses beautiful Jain temples carved from marble and sandstone.
  • The fort has witnessed several historic battles and was once a key trade route stop.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Best explored early morning or late afternoon for photography.
  • Carry water and light clothing — desert heat can be intense.
  • Don’t miss the Jain Temples and the Maharaja’s Palace inside the fort.
  • Nearby attractions: Patwon Ki Haveli, Sam Sand Dunes, Gadisar Lake, and Bada Bagh.
