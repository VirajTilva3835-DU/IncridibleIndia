Gateway of India, Mumbai (Maharashtra)

"Historic arch monument overlooking the Arabian Sea, built during British rule."

About the Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is an iconic monument situated on the waterfront of Colaba, Mumbai, overlooking the Arabian Sea. It was built in 1924 to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911. Designed by British architect George Wittet, it represents the grandeur of India’s colonial history and serves as a symbolic entry point to the country.


Constructed from yellow basalt and reinforced concrete, the Gateway combines elements of Hindu and Muslim architectural styles. It became a ceremonial and historical landmark, most notably as the site where the last British troops departed India in 1948, marking the end of British rule.


Interesting Facts

  • The foundation stone was laid in 1913, and the structure was completed in 1924.
  • It stands 26 meters (85 feet) tall and faces the Arabian Sea.
  • The last British soldiers to leave India passed through the Gateway in 1948.
  • The design blends Indo-Saracenic, Gujarati, and Islamic architectural styles.
  • It is one of Mumbai’s most photographed landmarks, especially at sunrise and sunset.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Visit early morning for peaceful views and fewer crowds.
  • Try the ferry ride to Elephanta Caves from the Gateway pier.
  • Best photo opportunities are at sunrise and sunset.
  • Nearby attractions: Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, Colaba Causeway Market, Marine Drive, and Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus.
