Gir National Park, Gujarat

"Only home of the Asiatic lions, offering jeep safaris through dry deciduous forest."

About Gir National Park

Gir National Park, located in the Junagadh district of Gujarat, is the only natural habitat of the Asiatic lions in the world. Established in 1965, it spans over 1,400 square kilometers and is a treasure trove of biodiversity, featuring dry deciduous forests, grasslands, hills, and rivers.


The park is not only famous for its majestic lions but also home to a rich variety of wildlife including leopards, deer, antelopes, crocodiles, and over 300 species of birds. Jeep safaris through Gir offer an unforgettable experience to witness these creatures in their natural habitat.


Interesting Facts

  • Gir is the only place in the world where Asiatic lions can be seen in the wild.
  • The park houses over 600 Asiatic lions as per the latest census.
  • Gir also supports around 2,000 species of fauna, making it a biodiversity hotspot.
  • Devalia Safari Park serves as a mini version of Gir for quicker wildlife sightings.
  • It is one of the best-managed wildlife sanctuaries in India, with successful conservation efforts since 1965.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Book your safari tickets online in advance through the official Gir Forest website.
  • Carry binoculars and cameras with zoom lenses for best wildlife viewing.
  • Wear light-colored cotton clothes and avoid strong perfumes.
  • Nearby attractions: Somnath Temple, Junagadh Fort, and Devalia Safari Park.
