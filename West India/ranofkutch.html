Rann of Kutch, Gujarat

"Vast white salt desert famous for the Rann Utsav festival and beautiful sunsets."

About Rann of Kutch

The Rann of Kutch is a spectacular white salt desert located in the Kutch district of Gujarat, stretching across thousands of square kilometers near the India–Pakistan border. Known for its surreal landscape that glows under the moonlight, it is one of the most mesmerizing natural wonders of India.


Every year, the region hosts the famous Rann Utsav — a cultural festival celebrating the rich art, music, and traditions of Gujarat. Visitors can enjoy folk dances, camel rides, local crafts, and luxury tent stays right on the desert under starry skies. The Rann offers a unique blend of culture, color, and serenity.


Interesting Facts

  • The Rann spans over 7,500 sq km, making it one of the largest salt deserts in the world.
  • During monsoon, the desert gets submerged under water and reappears as a salt flat when dry.
  • The Rann Utsav is celebrated from November to February every year.
  • The desert appears magical under the full moonlight.
  • The area is also home to the Indian Wild Ass Sanctuary in Little Rann of Kutch.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Book accommodation in advance during Rann Utsav.
  • Carry warm clothes — desert nights can be very cold.
  • Don’t miss the White Desert Viewpoint and Kalo Dungar (Black Hill) for panoramic views.
  • Explore nearby attractions like Bhuj, Mandvi Beach, and India Bridge.
  • Visit during Full Moon nights for the most breathtaking views.
