About Shirdi Sai Baba Temple

The Shirdi Sai Baba Temple in Maharashtra is one of India’s most revered pilgrimage sites, dedicated to Sai Baba of Shirdi — a saint who preached love, tolerance, and the unity of all religions. Located in the small town of Shirdi in the Ahmednagar district, this temple attracts millions of devotees from across the globe every year.


The main shrine, known as the Samadhi Mandir, houses the marble idol and tomb of Sai Baba. Devotees visit to seek his blessings, offer prayers, and experience the divine peace that surrounds the temple complex. Shirdi stands as a beacon of spirituality, symbolizing faith (“Shraddha”) and patience (“Saburi”) — Sai Baba’s core teachings.


Interesting Facts

  • Sai Baba lived in Shirdi for over 50 years, performing miracles and guiding people toward moral and spiritual living.
  • The temple was built in 1922 over Sai Baba’s samadhi (final resting place).
  • Over 25,000 devotees visit the temple daily, with numbers exceeding 100,000 during festivals.
  • Major festivals celebrated here include Rama Navami, Guru Purnima, and Vijayadashami.
  • The temple trust operates free meals, accommodation, and healthcare services for devotees.

Travel Tips & Nearby Attractions

  • Attend the daily Aartis for a divine experience — Kakad Aarti (early morning) is highly recommended.
  • Electronic devices are restricted inside the Samadhi Mandir — lockers are available outside.
  • Try the free Prasadalaya meals organized by the temple trust.
  • Nearby attractions: Dwarkamai Mosque, Khandoba Temple, and Shani Shingnapur Temple (approx. 70 km away).
