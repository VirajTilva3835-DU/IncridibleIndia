Hawa Mahal, Jaipur (Rajasthan)

"The iconic 'Palace of Winds' — a masterpiece of Rajput architecture."

About the Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is one of Jaipur’s most famous landmarks. Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, this unique five-storey structure was designed by architect Lal Chand Ustad. It is made of red and pink sandstone and blends beautifully with the city’s vibrant architecture.


The palace features 953 small windows (jharokhas) decorated with intricate latticework. These windows allowed royal women to observe street festivals and daily life without being seen by the public, maintaining the purdah system. The design also allows cool air to circulate, keeping the interiors comfortable even during the summer — hence its name, "Palace of Winds."


Interesting Facts

  • Hawa Mahal has no foundation — it stands upright because of its curved architecture and pyramid shape.
  • There are 953 jharokhas (windows) designed to allow airflow and natural cooling.
  • The palace was built for royal women to view city life unseen.
  • It is constructed from red and pink sandstone, typical of Jaipur’s architecture.
  • The top floors are reached by ramps instead of stairs for easier access.
